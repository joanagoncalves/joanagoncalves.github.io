Required background (composing your study programme)

For a thesis in this lab, students should have background knowledge in one or more of the following disciplines (at least 2 courses):

  • Algorithmics and Complexity (e.g. BSc courses CSE1305 / CSE2310 / CSE2315, MSc course IN4301)
  • Machine Learning and Data Science (e.g. BSc course CSE2510, MSc courses CS4220 / CS4070)

In addition, we expect:

  • Good programming skills.
  • Knowledge of university-level mathematics: Calculus (e.g. BSc CSE1200), Linear Algebra (e.g. BSc CSE1205), Probability and Statistics (e.g. BSc CSE1210).

This background is typically acquired through BSc + MSc programmes in Computer Science.

For Computer Science MSc students, a good start for the research is to do a 7-8 week literature survey (IN4306, 10EC). This consists in writing a review of the state-of-the-art in the chosen research area. Following the MSc Bioinformatics specialization is strongly recommended for research topics delving deeper into the application domain, but not mandatory for research topics on more fundamental computational challenges with associated applications in bioinformatics.

Students following other programmes, such as Nanobiology or Life Science and Technology, are advised to follow the above courses as electives in their programmes, or to follow a minor in Computer Science.



To apply for a thesis with us:

  1. Make sure you have the required background (see above).
  2. Browse the list of potential topics to see whether your interests align with ours, and to identify your preferences (see below).
  3. Send an email to make an appointment to discuss possibilities. Please attach your study plan or study transcripts, including the list of all the courses you have taken.
